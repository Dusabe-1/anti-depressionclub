Overview

What is depression? A Mayo Clinic expert explains.

Depression is a mood disorder that causes a persistent feeling of sadness and loss of interest. Also called major depressive disorder or clinical depression, it affects how you feel, think and behave and can lead to a variety of emotional and physical problems. You may have trouble doing normal day-to-day activities, and sometimes you may feel as if life isn't worth living.

Overview

Symptoms of depression in men



1.Emotional Changes: Men may experience sadness, hopelessness, or emptiness, but these feelings may be masked by irritability, anger, or aggressive outbursts.

2.Behavioral Changes: Men may withdraw from social activities, neglect hobbies, spend excessive time at work or on sports, or engage in escapist behaviors like excessive alcohol or drug use.

3.Physical Symptoms: Depression can manifest as physical symptoms like headaches, digestive problems, pain, or sleep disturbances.

4.Loss of Interest: Men may lose interest in previously enjoyable activities, including sex, hobbies, or sports.

5.Changes in Appetite and Sleep: Changes in appetite (eating more or less) and sleep patterns (insomnia or sleeping too much) are common.

6.Risky Behaviors: Men may engage in risky behaviors like reckless driving or impulsive decisions.

7.Changes in Sexuality: Depression can lead to decreased libido, erectile dysfunction, or orgasmic dysfunction.

8.Suicidal Thoughts: While men may not attempt suicide as often as women, they are more likely to die by suicide if they attempt it.

9.Difficulty Concentrating: Men may struggle to concentrate, make decisions, or remember things.
